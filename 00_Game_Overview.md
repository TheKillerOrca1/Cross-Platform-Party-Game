## The Pitch
A cross-platform, asymmetric multiplayer party game where each device (Mobile, VR, PC, Console) plays a fundamentally different but perfectly balanced role in the same shared match. Instead of every platform playing the same game with different controls, each platform's unique hardware strengths are turned into a genuinely unique playstyle — the touchscreen, the joystick, the mouse, and full 3D presence all become distinct competitive advantages that only make sense on their platform.

Think: **the scope and "everyone plays differently" magic of Acron: Attack of the Squirrels**, combined with the readable, punchy, competitive art direction of **Brawl Stars meets Overwatch**, wrapped in a **silly fantasy world with wholesome chaos energy** — but played at an **intense, edge-of-your-seat competitive pace**, not a purely goofy party-game pace.

## Core Design Pillars

1. **Platform Identity, Not Platform Parity** — Mobile, VR, PC, and Console are not the same game with different control schemes. Each is a genuinely different playstyle that leverages what that hardware is uniquely good at.
2. **Balanced Asymmetry** — Every platform should feel powerful and viable. Difficulty should come from *character choice*, not from which device you own.
3. **Wholesome Chaos, Competitive Intensity** — The tone is silly and fun (goofy fantasy world, absurd flavor), but the *gameplay* should feel tense, skillful, and competitive — not lighthearted button-mashing.
4. **Shifting Allegiances** — Teams are not fixed. Player-chosen or round-based team assignment supports multiple future gamemodes (FFA, boss-vs-all, co-op) beyond the initial CTF mode.
5. **Silly Fantasy, Not Generic Fantasy** — The world should feel like it has its own personality (see Art Direction doc) — not a serious high-fantasy setting, but not a joke setting either.

## Target Feel
- **Fast-paced and tense**, not slow or purely casual
- **Readable at a glance** across every platform (phone screen to VR headset to TV)
- **Genuinely competitive** — matches should feel close and skill should matter
- **Approachable but deep** — easy to understand the core loop, hard to master individual characters

## Reference Points
- **Acron: Attack of the Squirrels** — asymmetric cross-platform structure (VR vs. mobile)
- **Brawl Stars** — bold outlines, chunky silhouettes, poppy readable art
- **Overwatch** — grounded proportions, competitive character design, ability-based combat feel
- **Onward / VR shooters** — deliberate, tactical ranged combat feel for VR
- **Superhot / melee VR games** — dynamic, physical, dodge-driven melee feel for VR

## Players & Session Length
- **3–8+ players** (scalable, potential for larger lobbies later)
- **Quick rounds**: ~6 minutes per round
- **Best-of-3 captures** format (see Gamemode doc for full details)
- **Teams**: Chosen manually by players by default; random shuffle available as an option each round

## Platforms (Core 4, Launch Scope)
- **Mobile**
- **VR**
- **PC**
- **Console**

*(Web-only Spectator Mode and future platforms like Nintendo Switch 2 are noted as future scope — see Backlog doc.)*

## What This Game Is NOT
- Not a purely goofy/silly party game with no stakes (e.g., not Ultimate Chicken Horse tier chaos)
- Not a game where one platform is objectively best or easiest
- Not anime-styled — competitive/stylized art direction (see Art Direction doc)
