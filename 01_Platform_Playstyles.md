# Platform Playstyles

Each platform gets **two selectable playstyles** at the start of a match. Difficulty/skill ceiling is tied to *character/playstyle choice*, not to which platform you're on — all platforms should feel balanced against each other overall.

---

## 📱 Mobile — "Numbers & Control"
**Core identity**: Leverage the touchscreen's ability to quickly tap/drag across multiple discrete units — something that feels clunky on mouse/stick but natural on touch.

### Option A: Squad Leader
- One strong central unit, supported by weaker minion units
- Minions can be summoned/directed with taps and swipes
- Playstyle: command-and-control, positioning your leader safely while minions do supporting work
- Fantasy flavor: a spellcaster commanding small conjured creatures

### Option B: Swappable Squad (2–3 units)
- Control 2–3 roughly equal units, tap to instantly switch which one you're actively controlling
- Encourages multitasking and split-second decisions about which unit to prioritize
- Playstyle: juggling multiple threats/positions at once, using touchscreen's quick-tap advantage to "be in two places at once"

### Design Notes
- Touch input should feel like its own unique advantage — rapid unit-switching or multi-touch gestures (e.g., drag to move one unit while tapping to command another) should NOT be replicable well on PC/Console
- Open question: can minions attack, or are they utility/blocking only? *(To be tested in playtesting)*

---

## 🥽 VR — "Physical Presence"
**Core identity**: Full 3D embodiment and physicality — dodging, aiming, and movement should feel like it's actually your body reacting, not a joystick abstraction.

### Option A: Melee (faster)
- Inspired by SuperHot-style dynamic melee — dodging, weaving, precise physical timing
- Faster movement speed than Ranged
- Close-quarters combat: swords, fists, or magical melee weapons
- Rewards spatial awareness and physical reflexes

### Option B: Ranged (magic caster)
- Inspired by Onward-style deliberate, tactical shooter feel — but with **magic** instead of guns
- Uses cover, positioning, and aim precision
- "Reload" equivalent: a tactile, deliberate magic-charging or component-gathering action that mimics the tension of reloading a gun in a tactical shooter, but fits the fantasy theme
- **Not slow** — should still feel dynamic and active, just more deliberate/tactical than Melee rather than sluggish

### Design Notes
- Both VR options can carry/capture the flag/relic (all platforms can)
- Melee = faster & more reflex-based; Ranged = tactical & precision-based (not "slow," just different tempo)

## 🖱️ PC — "Precision & Damage" (Assassin/Damage Archetype)
**Core identity**: High-precision mouse aim and the versatility of a full keyboard's worth of inputs — leaned into as burst damage and skillful positioning/aim.

- Archetype: **Assassin / Damage dealer**
- Leverages precise mouse-based aiming and/or targeting for high-skill-ceiling damage output
- Likely benefits from keyboard's many simultaneous inputs — abilities, quick reflexes, combos
- *(Two specific playstyle options within this archetype still TBD — to be fleshed out in next design pass, e.g., a burst-damage assassin vs. a sustained-damage duelist)*

### Design Notes
- PC's advantage = precision + input versatility, expressed as damage/skill-expression rather than raw survivability
- Should feel high-skill-ceiling: rewards good aim and good decision-making under pressure

---

## 🎮 Console — "Fluid Support" (Support Archetype)
**Core identity**: The analog stick's strength in smooth, continuous movement — leaned into as mobility-based support and utility rather than raw damage.

- Archetype: **Support / Mobility-based utility**
- Leverages joystick fluidity for buffing allies, repositioning, area utility, or movement-based support abilities
- Should feel "smooth" and satisfying to move — the kind of flow that's specifically hard to replicate with mouse/touch
- *(Two specific playstyle options within this archetype still TBD — e.g., a buffer/healer vs. a battlefield-repositioning support)*

### Design Notes
- Console's advantage = fluid, continuous movement — this should translate into support gameplay that feels great to move around with, not just stat-buffing from a fixed position

## Platform Identity Summary Table

| Platform | Archetype | Strength Leveraged | Tempo |
|----------|-----------|---------------------|-------|
| Mobile | Squad Command / Multi-unit Control | Touchscreen multi-tap/swipe | Medium-fast, multitasking |
| VR | Physical Combat (Melee or Ranged) | 3D embodiment, physical presence | Fast (melee) / Tactical (ranged) |
| PC | Assassin / Damage | Mouse precision, input versatility | High-skill burst |
| Console | Fluid Support | Analog stick smoothness | Continuous, flowing |
