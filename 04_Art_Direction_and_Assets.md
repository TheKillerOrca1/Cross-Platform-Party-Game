# Art Direction & Assets

## Overall Art Style
**"Brawl Stars meets Overwatch"**

- **From Brawl Stars**: bold black outlines, chunky/exaggerated proportions, super clean and readable silhouettes, poppy saturated colors
- **From Overwatch**: more grounded anatomy underneath the stylization, added material/texture detail (armor, fabric, magical effects), slightly more mature character design than a purely cutesy style
- **Explicitly NOT anime-inspired** — bold outlines + grounded proportions read as "competitive stylized game art," not anime. This is an intentional choice.

### Why This Style Fits the Game
- Needs to be **readable at a glance** across every platform — a phone screen, a VR headset, a TV, and a monitor simultaneously. Bold outlines + high-contrast silhouettes (Brawl Stars' core strength) directly solve this.
- Needs to feel **competitive and intense**, not purely cutesy — Overwatch's grounded proportions and character weight help ground the silliness in something that still feels like a "real" competitive game.

## Tone & Theme
- **Silly fantasy** — not generic high-fantasy, not a joke setting either. A world that has its own personality and sense of humor without undercutting the competitive stakes.
- **Wholesome chaos** — silly, fun, and inviting, but the gameplay itself should still feel tense and skillful (see Overview doc).

## The "Flag" Reimagined — The Relic
- The captured object is explicitly **NOT a literal flag** — it should be a **silly, thematic object** that fits the fantasy world's personality
- **Key design decision**: The object is a **magical Relic** at the mechanical/gameplay level (consistent function every match), but its **appearance is a cosmetic skin players can choose/equip** — meaning it could look like a potato, a cursed sandwich, a rubber chicken, an ancient sock, etc., depending on what's equipped
- This solves two problems at once:
  1. Gives the game a distinctive, funny visual identity instead of a generic flag
  2. Creates a natural cosmetic/monetization or personalization hook (players "equip" their preferred silly Relic skin) without affecting gameplay balance
- **Open creative direction**: brainstorm a roster of possible Relic skins that fit the "silly fantasy" theme (needs a dedicated brainstorm pass — see Backlog)

## Weapons & Magic Items (July 9, 2026 session)

- **No platform uses literal firearms — hard rule.** Every ranged weapon across every platform is a fantasy-reskinned equivalent: magic wands, staves, and similar. This includes PC's prototype "mouse-aimed attack," which is a fantasy ranged weapon, not a gun. Weapon silhouettes can borrow *handling* language from firearms (grips, bracing, aiming posture) but must read as magical objects, not reskinned rifles.
- **VR's wand is a two-handed, gun-like fantasy weapon** — thicker and visually substantial, aimed with both hands. It should look like something with real presence in your grip, not a twig.
- **Mana cells are physical props**: the VR reload ritual (eject a depleted cell via a wand-hand button → grab a fresh cell from a side pouch → slap it in → a second channel/charge motion) means depleted/fresh mana cells, the side pouch, and the charging state all need readable visual designs. The charge-up state especially should be visible to *other players* too (a player mid-reload is a gameplay-relevant tell).
- **Every magic item type gets its own unique reload/priming ritual** — the ritual is part of the item's visual and physical identity, so each new item design should include its gesture concept, not just its silhouette.

## Character Design Direction (Per Platform)
Visual identity should reinforce each platform's mechanical identity (see Platform Playstyles doc — identities updated July 9, 2026):

| Platform | Identity | Visual Direction Ideas |
|----------|-----------|-------------------------|
| Mobile | Direct Control + Gesture Movement | Single agile character; swooping wind/magic trail effects during traced dashes (the dash canonically reads as "a gust of magic wind carried them through the drawn shape") |
| VR | Physical Presence | Substantial two-handed wand silhouette, visible side pouch + mana cells on the rig, charging/priming effects; melee/sword silhouette ideas deferred until the weapon kit is decided |
| PC | Precision & Damage | Sleek, precise-feeling silhouette — sharper lines, implies speed and precision; ranged weapon reads as arcane instrument, never a gun |
| Console | Parkour Combat | Silhouette that reads as mid-motion agility — momentum trails, flowing elements that emphasize slide/wall-run/vault chains |

## Environment & Movement Affordances (flag for map session)
Movement mechanics locked this session put *readability requirements* on environment art, to be designed properly in the upcoming dedicated map session:
- Runnable walls, rails, and vaultable terrain (Console), fling-climbable walls (VR), and swoopable wall surfaces (Mobile) must be **visually identifiable at a glance**, in the same shared spaces, across a phone screen and a VR headset alike
- No visual language has been chosen for these affordances yet — open question for the map/environment art pass

## UI/UX Considerations
- UI must scale/adapt cleanly across a tiny phone screen, a VR headset HUD, a TV (10-foot UI for console), and a PC monitor
- Bold, high-contrast UI elements matching the bold-outline art style will help here too
- **Mobile control UI (decided July 9, 2026)**: dual virtual sticks plus a gesture zone that sits above them. The gesture zone must be **see-through/transparent** — visually locatable, but never blocking the player's view of the game world. The traced gesture should render as visible feedback while drawing
- Mobile joystick/gesture-zone size and sensitivity settings are planned as accessibility/comfort options (post-first-pass)
